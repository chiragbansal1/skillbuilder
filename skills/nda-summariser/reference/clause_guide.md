# NDA Clause Reference Guide

## Common clause types and what to look for

### Mutual vs One-way NDAs
- **Mutual**: Both parties share confidential information — obligations apply to both sides
- **One-way**: Only one party discloses — only the receiving party has obligations
- Flag if the NDA type is not explicitly stated

### Definition of Confidential Information
- Should be clearly defined — vague definitions favour the disclosing party
- Watch for overly broad definitions like "all information shared verbally or in writing"
- Exclusions should be listed: publicly available info, independently developed info,
  info received from third parties

### Duration
- Typical range: 1–5 years for commercial NDAs
- Perpetual confidentiality obligations are unusual — flag them
- Check whether obligations survive termination of the agreement

### Permitted Disclosures
- Standard carve-outs: legal/regulatory requirements, court orders
- Check if disclosure to employees/contractors is permitted and under what conditions
- Any disclosure to competitors should be flagged immediately

### Return or Destruction of Information
- Should specify what happens to confidential materials when the NDA ends
- "Destroy or return within X days" is standard
- Certification of destruction is stronger than a simple obligation to destroy

### High-risk clauses to flag
- No time limit on confidentiality
- No carve-out for publicly available information
- Unusually broad definition of confidential information
- Jurisdiction outside the firm's home country
- Penalties or liquidated damages clauses
- Assignment of IP created using the confidential information
